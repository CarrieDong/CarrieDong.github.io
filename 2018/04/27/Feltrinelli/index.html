01.Research Part

The research is diveded into 2 parts: Desk research and User research.
I actively suggested that we should collect the new trends of lifestyle, preparing for the innovation inspiration.
So when other group members are gathering the brand data like vision/mission, I read the MKC reports about customer trends in global and chinese markets, and induct into 3 pieces.

In order to decide the general direction of the survey, I went to visit the bookstore alone, did 3 shadowing researches, conducted 5-min interview for each shadowing and share the photos/voice recording among group.

Later, our group discussed the survey direction together, based on the various informations we gain, and started the survey phase.
Due to my background of service design, I actively took the task of listing the survey questions (in total 10, in terms of the shopping experience). Each of us are ought to complete 5 interviews, each of which cost 10min. In total we got 30 interviews finally.

02.Solution Development Part

This project is based on ‘design-thinking’ method, which means it follows the ‘problem-solving’ model, so the focus is on defining the problem.

Another 3 members and me were inducting the data that we got from the interviews, working in pairs. Then another guy calculated the statistic and express them in sheet. At last, we got 6 kinds of problem. But based on our capability and the feasibility, we defined one main problem: users lack the sufficient access to information, including book recommendation/instore book guidance/book preview/book delievery.
For the solution, as the class required, we planned to conduct via app.

1)about book recommendation: at first, one collegue suggest adding advertisement on page, while I suggest building community. So that we can make full use of user experience, let users recommend each other, and develop the feeling of engagement. This also inspired another collegue about GoodRead, an existing reading community. Consequently, our idea is to collaborate with GoodRead, use the community for recommendation except the existing advertisement.

2)about book guidance: in the beginning, someone put the idea of magnetic code. But its cost is too high to conduct. Luckily we found the operation system of liabrary is very smart, but it requires the human arrangement…we were close to give up. Then we went to bookstore again to get consult from Feltrinelli staff: the headquarter makes arrangement rules on specific shelves/sections, the staff have freedom to decide which row should the book be put on. Some of the staff choose to arrange in the order of the book name while some of the author. So the solution is: we made united rules on how to arrange in the row, so that we could guide customers to the place where the book ought to be, not the live location.

3)about preview: last semester, I joint a project which takes the technique of AR, so I suggested to use AR by scanning the QR code on the book. Another colleague preferred Google Lens. But after checking the complexicity and feasibility, we return back to QR code.

4)about delievery: easily learn from shopping app

5)In addition, some other colleagues suggested Chatbot feature, use the AI assistant to gain infor. We also discuss the promotion proposal, like get rewards by collecting 200 likes in instagram.

03.Prototype and Test Part

Still due to my background of service design, of course I took the responsibility of making the mock-up. Another colleague assisted me of picking the fundamental features. Then I add the search/instore digital payment/review management and instore map for guidance. This is the Mock-up.

About the test part, we planned to visit bookstore again, invite some friendly customers and staff to try our mock-up. Maybe we could collect some useful feedback which could stimulate our next iteration. But to be honest, this is just my theory, we are going to discuss our test proposal next week. After the test and iteration, we would publish new journey map and system map, including backstages and other touchpoints.

La Feltrinelli书店的基于design-thinking的创新战略

Feltrinelli书店是意大利最负盛名的实体书店连锁品牌，在电子商务时代背景下也开发了自己的网页和app端。
我们组的目的是通过调研发现用户的未被满足的需求并提出创新战略，应课程要求，产出要与电子端有关。

1.调研部分

分为desk调研和用户调研。
我主动建议同时要收集当下趋势的资料，为创新作准备。
于是其他组员在整理vision/mission等品牌价值的时候，我翻阅了麦肯锡和其他机构发布的全球和基于中国市场的用户趋势报告，并整理成3大条。
因为需要讨论大概解决什么方面的问题，我又独自前往书店做了三个shadowing research，并进行了各自5分钟的采访，整理成照片和录音。
在之后，大家根据各自渠道得到的信息，大概规划了问题方向，进入用户调研部分。
用户调研时，因为我服务设计的学习经历，我主动列出采访大纲，共计10个大问题，依照买书前/中/后的顺序。每名组员完成5个采访任务，每个需要10分钟。共计完成30个采访。

2.方案发展部分

此次项目的基于design-thinking是指由需求出发，发展方案，所以项目重点是确定问题。
从第一部分得到的采访由我和另外3名同学整理，两人一组，将各问题的回答归纳，再由另一人计算数据，用图表形式表现。
结果共确定6个问题，基于我们的能力和实现难道，我们共同确定了一个大问题：缺乏得到足够信息的能力(提了好多小问题，我讨论时候灵感一来就归纳出来了)。
主要包括：书推荐信息不合理，书店内不好找书，书店内无法全部预览，线上购买后快递信息不知情。
在解决思路上，应课程要求，落地点是app。
1）所以书推荐信息：一开始组员想到是增加广告，我想到发展社区，根据用户的经验相互推荐。
这启发了另一个同学推荐了GoodRead，一个成型的读书社区，最终我们确定利用品牌间合作

2）找书：一开始有同学推荐利用磁条码，但是成本高不合适。后来我们想到学习图书馆系统，但是需要人工整理，即将要放弃。我们又去了一趟书店咨询工作人员，得知书店摆放只要求到某个书架，摆放的排数是工作人员自己的习惯决定，有的按书名有的按作者，所以我们商量的办法是，书店规范摆放要求，搜索结果显示该书应该摆放的位置，不是实时位置。

3）书店预览：我上学期的另一个项目有用到过AR技术，所以直接建议添加QRcode，另外同学建议GoogleLens，最终另一个同学从技术实现角度仔细分析，还是选择了QRcode

4）快递信息：借鉴购物app的快递通知系统，不过多讨论。

5）另外有同学建议添加chatbot的功能，人工智能助手找书。我们还共同讨论了推广方案，比如点赞数最多得到折扣等等。

3.落地

第二部分确定之后，我由于出身理所当然地负责完成原型。另一位同学协助我确定了app的基础功能，我又添加了搜索/店内电子支付/管理书评，
和利用高德室内地图的店内找书功能。原型在这里。
关于test部分，我们打算前往书店邀请读者和工作人员试用app原型，再根据建议迭代。目前只到原型部分，test下周计划。
当test结束，方案确定之后，我们将会发布全新的journey map和system map，包括后台等touch point。
